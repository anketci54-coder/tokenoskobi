## SECTION 4: AUTHORITY PHILOSOPHY

Tokenoskobi authority is designed as a constrained hierarchy that protects:
- safety,
- explainability,
- accountability,
- institutional continuity.

Authority philosophy anchors:
- **Authority is granted, never assumed.**
- **Authority is scoped, never unlimited.**
- **Authority is reviewable, never opaque.**
- **Authority is constitutional, never ad hoc.**

## SECTION 5: ROLE HIERARCHY

Constitutional hierarchy:

1. **Başkomutan (final constitutional authority)**
2. **Guardian (constitutional hard enforcement)**
3. **Risk Authority (risk veto and risk gating)**
4. **Harekât Subayı (AI orchestration and recommendation synthesis)**
5. **Uzmanlar Konseyi / Uzman (specialist intelligence roles)**
6. **Operator (human approval and operational gate action)**
7. **System Constraints Layer (immutable non-negotiable bounds)**

Hierarchy doctrine:
- lower roles cannot override upper constitutional authority,
- upper roles remain bound by constitutional restrictions,
- system constraints remain active across all hierarchy levels.

## SECTION 7: PERMISSION LEVELS

Permission levels are constitutional labels:

- **P0: PROHIBITED**
  - Role has no authority in this scope.
- **P1: VIEW**
  - Role can inspect and reference only.
- **P2: RECOMMEND**
  - Role can propose but cannot approve or execute final action.
- **P3: APPROVE_WITHIN_SCOPE**
  - Role can approve only in bounded domain.
- **P4: VETO_WITHIN_SCOPE**
  - Role can block action in bounded domain.
- **P5: CONSTITUTIONAL_OVERRIDE_PATH**
  - Restricted to constitutional procedure, not discretionary operation.

---

## SECTION 8: APPROVAL MATRIX

Approval matrix doctrine:
- all high-impact actions require explicit approval chain,
- recommendation is not approval,
- approval is not constitutional override.

Baseline approval path:
1. Specialist inputs completed
2. Harekât Subayı synthesis produced
3. Risk context resolved
4. Guardian compliance validated
5. Operator approval gate decision
6. Constitutional constraints rechecked

No approval path can bypass Guardian compliance or constitutional restrictions.

## SECTION 9: GUARDIAN AUTHORITY

Guardian authority is absolute for constitutional enforcement.

Guardian powers:
- hard-block non-compliant operations,
- block authority misuse,
- block unauthorized delegation,
- block constitutional drift.

Guardian limitations:
- cannot erase constitutional audit trail,
- cannot redefine constitution outside amendment procedure.

Guardian veto priority is immediate and non-bypassable.

## SECTION 11: BAŞKOMUTAN AUTHORITY

Başkomutan authority:
- final constitutional authority,
- final amendment acceptance path,
- final constitutional interpretation direction.

Başkomutan constraints:
- constitutional process remains mandatory,
- must preserve immutability and audit doctrine,

Authority class: **P5 CONSTITUTIONAL_OVERRIDE_PATH** through formal process only.

## SECTION 12: SPECIALIST AUTHORITY

Specialist (Uzman) authority is domain-bounded intelligence authority.

Specialists can:
- assess evidence,
- provide expert analysis,
- produce risk-intelligence context,
- declare uncertainty and divergence.

Specialists cannot:
- issue final operational approval,
- issue constitutional override,
- suppress conflicting specialist views.

Authority class: **P2 RECOMMEND** with explicit accountability tags.

## SECTION 13: RUNTIME AUTHORITY

Runtime authority in constitutional scope is constrained by current zero-authority posture and controlled continuation doctrine.

Runtime authority rules:
- no autonomous expansion of authority,
- runtime behavior does not redefine role permissions,
- runtime context is subject to constitutional controls,
- runtime constraints remain subordinate to Guardian and constitutional doctrine.

## SECTION 14: PAPER TRADING AUTHORITY

Paper Trading authority is simulation-scoped and non-live by constitutional doctrine.

Paper Trading authority includes:
- simulation approval pathways,
- observational outcome capture,
- learning-support outputs.

Paper Trading authority excludes:
- live capital authority,
- live execution authority,
- hidden conversion to live mode.

## SECTION 15: SHADOW AUDIT AUTHORITY

Shadow Audit authority is verification authority without command substitution.

Shadow Audit can:
- validate consistency,
- detect drift,
- surface hidden contradictions,
- propose corrective governance actions.

Shadow Audit cannot:
- silently override approvals,
- mutate authoritative case truth,
- bypass constitutional escalation channels.

---

## SECTION 16: EMERGENCY AUTHORITY

Emergency authority is controlled under immutable hierarchy:
1. Guardian
2. Risk Authority
3. Harekât Subayı coordination
4. Operator containment actions
5. System constraints always active

Emergency doctrine:
- containment first,
- constitutional continuity preserved,
- post-emergency audit mandatory.

---

## SECTION 17: KILL SWITCH AUTHORITY

Kill Switch authority is emergency containment control.

Kill Switch governance:
- invocation must be auditable,
- invocation cannot disable guardian controls,
- invocation requires post-event review,
- deactivation requires constitutional readiness confirmation.

Kill Switch is a safety authority, not a discretionary control tool.

---

## SECTION 18: ESCALATION MATRIX

Escalation levels:

- **E1 Specialist Escalation:** unresolved specialist divergence
- **E2 Orchestration Escalation:** synthesis conflict or uncertainty breach
- **E3 Risk Escalation:** unacceptable risk posture
- **E4 Guardian Escalation:** constitutional non-compliance
- **E5 Emergency Escalation:** immediate containment required
- **E6 Constitutional Escalation:** amendment or doctrine-level conflict

Escalation rules:
- escalation path must be explicit,
- escalation owner must be explicit,
- escalation closure requires accountable decision record.

---

## SECTION 19: CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Conflict resolution order:
1. Evidence and scope clarification
2. Specialist divergence surfacing
3. Orchestration synthesis review
4. Risk authority gating
5. Guardian constitutional enforcement
6. Constitutional escalation path (if unresolved)

Conflict resolution principles:
- no hidden conflict suppression,
- no authority abuse to force false consensus,
- unresolved conflict must be recorded.

---

## SECTION 20: SEPARATION OF DUTIES

Separation of duties is mandatory.

Core separations:
- recommendation vs approval,
- approval vs constitutional enforcement,
- specialist analysis vs final authority,
- verification (shadow audit) vs command authority,
- emergency containment vs amendment authority.

No single role may collapse all control surfaces in one decision chain.

---

## SECTION 21: DELEGATION RULES

Delegation doctrine:
- delegation is explicit, scoped, and time/context bounded,
- delegated actions remain traceable,
- original authority owner remains accountable,
- delegation cannot transfer constitutional final authority,
- delegation cannot remove guardian controls.

Delegation invalid if:
- undocumented,
- outside scope,
- conflicting with constitutional restrictions.
